Light Sensor

The circuit is composed of a light sensor and a tone speaker, the Arduino reads the amount of light in the room and maps that data into a set of numbers, from that data the numbers are grouped into different tones. The output results in varying musical tones depending on the light received.

Light Sensor II

Similarly to the light receptor into tone circuit, the following circuit triggers the 180-degree servo. It’s achieved in the same manner with the Arduino mapping the light input, the information triggers the motor which turns 180 degrees.

Galvanic Response

The circuit above utilises galvanic skin response, we are conductors of electricity, we do so though the sweat and electrical conductivity of the skin. For this this circuit each cable is attached to a potato, orange, and aluminium foil, each of this conduct electricity differently, the more water it contains the more electricity it will conduct, in this case aluminium being a metal will also be a good conductor due to its atomic structure. By touching the circuit, we become part of the circuit, disturbing the conductivity, this disturbance is recognised by the Arduino which maps the electric current to a tone.
